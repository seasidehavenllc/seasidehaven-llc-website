Harassment & Workplace Conduct Policy
Purpose

Seaside Haven LLC is committed to maintaining a safe, respectful, and professional work environment. This policy outlines the company’s expectations regarding workplace conduct and establishes a zero‑tolerance stance toward harassment, discrimination, and abusive behavior.

1. Zero‑Tolerance Policy

Harassment, discrimination, bullying, intimidation, or any form of abusive behavior is strictly prohibited. Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

2. Forms of Harassment

Harassment includes any unwelcome behavior that creates a hostile, intimidating, or offensive work environment. This may include:
• Verbal harassment (insults, slurs, threats)
• Physical harassment (pushing, hitting, unwanted contact)
• Visual harassment (offensive gestures, images, or materials)
• Sexual harassment (unwanted advances, comments, or contact)
• Digital harassment (inappropriate messages, texts, or online conduct)

3. Discrimination

Discrimination based on race, color, religion, gender, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, or any protected characteristic is strictly prohibited.

All employees must treat coworkers, customers, and management with respect and professionalism.

4. Workplace Conduct Expectations

Employees must maintain professional behavior at all times. This includes:
• Using respectful language
• Avoiding confrontations
• Following supervisor instructions
• Maintaining a positive attitude
• Avoiding disruptive or hostile behavior

Employees must also follow all division‑specific conduct rules outlined in their division packet.

5. Reporting Harassment or Misconduct

Employees who experience or witness harassment or misconduct must report it immediately to management. Reports may be made verbally or in writing.

Retaliation against employees who report misconduct is strictly prohibited.

6. Investigation Process

All reports of harassment or misconduct will be investigated promptly and confidentially. Employees involved in an investigation must cooperate fully.

Appropriate corrective action will be taken based on the findings of the investigation.

7. Digital & Social Media Conduct

Harassment or misconduct through digital communication—including texts, social media, messaging apps, or job‑tracking platforms—is treated the same as in‑person misconduct.

Employees must follow all digital communication rules outlined in the Digital Communication & Device Policy.
