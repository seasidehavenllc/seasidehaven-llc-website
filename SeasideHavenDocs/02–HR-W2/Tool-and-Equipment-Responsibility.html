Tool & Equipment Responsibility Agreement
Purpose

This agreement outlines the responsibilities of all W2 employees regarding the use, care, and handling of company tools, equipment, and vehicles. Proper use and maintenance are essential for safety, productivity, and cost control across all divisions of Seaside Haven LLC.

1. Proper Use of Tools & Equipment

Employees must use tools and equipment only for their intended purpose and in accordance with company training and safety guidelines. This includes:
• Following all safety procedures
• Using tools only if trained and authorized
• Inspecting tools before use
• Reporting unsafe or damaged tools immediately

Employees may not modify, alter, or disable safety guards or features on any equipment.

2. Care & Maintenance

Employees are responsible for basic care and upkeep of tools and equipment assigned to them, including:
• Cleaning tools after use
• Storing tools properly
• Keeping equipment dry and protected
• Not leaving tools unattended on job sites

Any tool or equipment found damaged due to negligence may result in disciplinary action.

3. Loss, Theft, or Damage

Employees must immediately report any lost, stolen, or damaged tools or equipment. Failure to report incidents promptly may result in disciplinary action.

If an employee’s negligence results in loss or damage, the employee may be held financially responsible depending on the severity and circumstances.

4. Company Vehicles

Employees authorized to operate company vehicles must:
• Follow all traffic laws
• Maintain a clean interior
• Report mechanical issues immediately
• Never operate a vehicle under the influence
• Never allow unauthorized individuals to drive or ride

Any damage caused by reckless or unsafe driving may result in disciplinary action or termination.

5. Division-Specific Equipment

Each division may have specialized tools or equipment that require additional training or certification. Employees must follow all division-specific equipment rules provided in their division packet and may not operate specialized equipment without proper authorization.

6. Prohibited Actions

The following actions are strictly prohibited:
• Using tools for personal projects
• Taking tools home without permission
• Leaving tools unsecured
• Misusing or intentionally damaging equipment
• Allowing non-employees to use company tools
