Attendance & Punctuality Policy
Purpose

This policy outlines the attendance and punctuality expectations for all W2 employees of Seaside Haven LLC. Reliable attendance is essential for maintaining workflow, meeting customer expectations, and supporting team operations across all divisions.

1. Reporting to Work

Employees are expected to:
• Arrive on time
• Be prepared to work at the scheduled start time
• Report directly to the assigned job site or meeting location
• Clock in using Connecteam as required

Employees must follow all division-specific reporting procedures outlined in their division packet.

2. Tardiness

Employees are considered late if they arrive after their scheduled start time. Excessive tardiness disrupts workflow and may result in disciplinary action.

If an employee will be late, they must notify management as early as possible.

3. Absences

Employees must notify management before the start of their shift if they will be absent. Acceptable notification methods include:
• Phone call
• Text message
• Connecteam communication tools

Failure to notify management may result in disciplinary action.

4. No-Call / No-Show

A “no-call / no-show” occurs when an employee fails to report to work and does not notify management. This is considered a serious violation and may result in immediate disciplinary action or termination.

5. Early Departures

Employees must notify management if they need to leave work early. Unauthorized early departures may result in disciplinary action.

6. Job Site Attendance

Employees must remain on the assigned job site unless instructed otherwise by management. Leaving a job site without permission is prohibited.

Employees must clock out when leaving the job site unless otherwise directed.

7. Division-Specific Attendance Rules

Some divisions may have additional attendance requirements based on job type, scheduling, or customer needs. Employees must follow all division-specific attendance rules outlined in their division packet.

8. Disciplinary Action

Attendance violations may result in:
• Verbal warnings
• Written warnings
• Suspension
• Termination

The severity of disciplinary action depends on the frequency and nature of the violation.
