Remodeling Division Safety Agreement
Purpose

This agreement outlines the safety requirements, PPE standards, and operational expectations for employees assigned to the Remodeling Division of Seaside Haven LLC. Remodeling work involves demolition, construction, cutting, dust, chemicals, and other hazards that require strict compliance with safety procedures.

Mandatory PPE Requirements

The following PPE is required at all times while performing remodeling work:
• Steel toe or composite toe boots
• Cut-resistant gloves
• Safety glasses
• Hard hat
• Respirator (dust, mold, demo, chemicals, fumes)
• Hearing protection (power tools, saws, demo equipment)

Additional PPE may be required depending on the job site, including face shields, chemical-resistant gloves, or specialized equipment.

Tool & Equipment Safety

Remodeling employees must:
• Inspect tools before use
• Use tools only if trained and authorized
• Never bypass safety guards
• Report damaged tools immediately
• Follow all manufacturer and company safety guidelines

Power tools may only be operated by employees who have completed required training.

Demolition Safety

Demolition work requires heightened awareness and strict compliance. Employees must:
• Wear respirators during demo
• Identify structural hazards before starting
• Follow supervisor instructions
• Maintain safe distance from active demo zones
• Avoid striking unknown surfaces

Employees must immediately report any suspected structural instability.

Dust, Mold, & Chemical Exposure

Remodeling work may involve exposure to hazardous materials. Employees must:
• Wear respirators when required
• Follow all SDS sheet instructions
• Avoid disturbing mold without proper PPE
• Use chemical-safe gloves when handling solvents or adhesives

Any exposure incident must be reported immediately.

Job Site Conduct

Remodeling employees must:
• Maintain a clean and organized work area
• Follow supervisor instructions
• Avoid horseplay or unsafe behavior
• Respect customer property
• Communicate professionally

Employees must follow all digital communication and photo upload requirements for their division.

Required Photo Documentation

Remodeling employees must upload:
• Before photos
• Progress photos
• After photos
• Damage or hazard documentation

Photos must be clear, accurate, and uploaded to the correct job folder using Connecteam, AccuLynx, or another approved platform.

Compliance

Failure to follow remodeling safety rules may result in:
• Removal from job sites
• Disciplinary action
• Suspension
• Termination

Safety violations are taken seriously due to the high-risk nature of remodeling work.
