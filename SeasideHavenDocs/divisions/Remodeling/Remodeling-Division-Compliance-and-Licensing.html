Remodeling Division Compliance & Licensing Requirements
Purpose

This document outlines the compliance, licensing, and training requirements for employees assigned to the Remodeling Division of Seaside Haven LLC. Remodeling work involves regulated tasks that require proper certification, training, and adherence to federal, state, and company standards.

1. Required Training for All Remodeling Employees

All remodeling employees must complete the following training before performing any work:
• Remodeling Division Safety Agreement
• PPE usage and hazard identification
• Tool and equipment operation training
• Job documentation and photo upload procedures
• Connecteam job tracking training

Additional training may be required depending on job type or customer requirements.

2. OSHA Compliance Requirements

Remodeling employees must follow all applicable OSHA standards, including:
• Fall protection
• Respiratory protection
• Hazard communication (SDS sheets)
• PPE requirements
• Power tool safety
• Ladder and scaffold safety

Employees must report unsafe conditions immediately and stop work if a hazard is identified.

3. Licensing Requirements for Certain Tasks

Some remodeling tasks require licensed professionals. Employees may NOT perform the following tasks unless they hold the appropriate license and have written approval from management:

Electrical Work:
• Live electrical work
• Wiring modifications
• Breaker panel work

Plumbing Work:
• Water line modifications
• Drain line modifications
• Fixture installation requiring plumbing disconnect

Structural Work:
• Load-bearing wall removal
• Beam installation
• Structural reinforcement

Employees without proper licensing may only perform non-licensed remodeling tasks.

4. Specialized Certifications

Certain tasks require additional certifications. Employees may NOT perform these tasks without proper certification:

Respirator Use:
• Requires fit test
• Requires respirator training

Mold Exposure Work:
• Requires mold awareness or remediation certification

Lead Paint Disturbance:
• Requires EPA Lead-Safe certification

Tile Installation (Advanced):
• Requires skill verification

5. Documentation Requirements

Remodeling employees must maintain accurate documentation, including:
• Training completion records
• Photo documentation (before, progress, after)
• Hazard reports
• Job notes and updates

All documentation must be uploaded to Connecteam, AccuLynx, or another approved platform.

6. Compliance Violations

Failure to follow compliance or licensing rules may result in:
• Removal from job sites
• Retraining requirements
• Disciplinary action
• Suspension
• Termination

Violations involving licensed work may result in immediate termination due to legal liability.
