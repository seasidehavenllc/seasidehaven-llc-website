Notary Policy
Purpose

This Notary Policy outlines the rules, responsibilities, and procedures for providing notarial services under Seaside Haven LLC. It ensures compliance with Virginia notarial laws and maintains consistent standards across all notarization activities.

Authorized Notarial Acts

Seaside Haven LLC notaries may perform the following acts in accordance with Virginia law:
• Acknowledgments
• Jurats
• Oaths and Affirmations
• Copy Certifications (where permitted)
• Remote Online Notarization (RON), if commissioned
• Other acts authorized by state statute

Identification Requirements

All signers must present valid, government‑issued photo identification. Acceptable forms include:
• Driver’s License
• State Identification Card
• Passport
• Military ID
• Other government‑issued ID with photo and signature

Identification must be current or within the legal grace period allowed by Virginia law.

Signer Requirements

• Signer must appear in person (unless using approved RON procedures)
• Signer must be willing and aware
• Signer must understand the document
• No signs of coercion, confusion, or impairment
• Signer must complete all required fields before notarization

Document Requirements

• Document must be complete and ready for signature
• No blank signature lines may remain
• Notary cannot provide legal advice or interpret document contents
• Notary may refuse documents that appear fraudulent or incomplete

Recordkeeping

• All notarizations must be logged in the official Notary Log
• Log entries must include date, time, signer info, document type, ID verification, and notarial act
• Remote notarizations must include audio/video session details
• Logs must be stored securely and retained according to Virginia requirements

Prohibited Actions

• Notary may not notarize their own signature
• Notary may not notarize documents for immediate family members
• Notary may not provide legal advice
• Notary may not backdate or alter notarial certificates
• Notary may not proceed if identification is invalid or expired

Fees

• Fees must comply with Virginia’s maximum allowable notary charges
• Travel fees may be charged separately and must be disclosed upfront
• Remote Online Notarization fees follow state guidelines

RON (Remote Online Notarization)

• Only notaries commissioned for RON may perform remote notarizations
• Identity verification must follow approved digital credential analysis
• Audio/video recording must be retained per state requirements
• Electronic signatures and seals must meet state security standards

Policy Enforcement

Failure to follow this policy may result in disciplinary action, suspension of notarial duties, or reporting to the Virginia Secretary of the Commonwealth.
