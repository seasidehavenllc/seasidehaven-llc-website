Confidentiality & Data Protection Agreement
Purpose

This agreement outlines the confidentiality and data protection requirements for all W2 employees of Seaside Haven LLC. Employees must protect company information, customer data, and internal procedures at all times.

1. Confidential Information

Confidential information includes, but is not limited to:
• Customer names, addresses, and contact information
• Job details, pricing, estimates, and invoices
• Internal procedures, workflows, and training materials
• Employee information
• Vendor and contractor information
• Business strategies, plans, and communications

Employees may not share, copy, distribute, or disclose confidential information to anyone outside the company unless authorized by management.

2. Customer Privacy

Employees must respect customer privacy at all times. This includes:
• Not discussing customer information with unauthorized individuals
• Not taking photos or videos of customer property without permission
• Not sharing job details on social media

Any breach of customer privacy may result in disciplinary action or termination.

3. Company Data Protection

Employees must protect company data by:
• Keeping documents secure
• Not leaving paperwork unattended
• Not sharing passwords or login credentials
• Reporting lost or stolen devices immediately

Employees may not access company systems or data beyond what is required for their assigned division.

4. Use of Technology

Employees must use company technology responsibly. This includes:
• Using devices only for work-related tasks
• Not installing unauthorized software
• Not accessing inappropriate websites
• Not transferring company data to personal devices

Employees must follow all digital communication and device policies provided by the company.

5. Division-Specific Confidentiality

Each division may have additional confidentiality requirements based on the nature of the work. Employees must follow all confidentiality rules outlined in their division packet and may not share division-specific procedures or methods with unauthorized individuals.

6. Termination of Employment

Confidentiality obligations continue even after employment ends. Former employees may not disclose or use any confidential information obtained during employment.
