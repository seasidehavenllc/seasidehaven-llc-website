Safety & PPE Requirements
Purpose

This document outlines the universal safety expectations and personal protective equipment (PPE) requirements for all W2 employees of Seaside Haven LLC. These standards apply to every employee regardless of division. Division-specific PPE requirements will be provided separately in each division’s packet.

1. General Safety Expectations

All employees must follow basic safety rules at all times, including:
• Maintaining awareness of surroundings
• Reporting hazards immediately
• Using tools and equipment properly
• Following supervisor instructions
• Keeping work areas clean and organized
• Avoiding horseplay or unsafe behavior

Employees must stop work immediately if a situation becomes unsafe and notify management.

2. Universal PPE Requirements

The following PPE requirements apply to all employees, regardless of division:

Mandatory for All Employees:
• Work boots (closed-toe, durable)
• Work gloves (general-purpose)
• Eye protection when using tools or chemicals
• Hearing protection when exposed to loud equipment
• Long pants
• Clothing appropriate for outdoor and physical work

Situational PPE (Required When Applicable):
• Respirators (dust, mold, chemicals, fumes)
• Face shields (grinding, cutting, debris)
• Chemical-resistant gloves
• Waterproof boots
• High-visibility vests

Division-specific PPE requirements will be provided in the employee’s division packet and must be followed at all times.

3. Equipment Safety

Employees must use company tools and equipment responsibly. This includes:
• Inspecting tools before use
• Reporting damaged or unsafe equipment
• Never bypassing safety guards
• Using equipment only for its intended purpose

Only trained and authorized employees may operate specialized equipment.

4. Chemical Safety

Employees must follow all chemical safety guidelines, including:
• Reading and following SDS sheets
• Wearing required PPE
• Properly storing chemicals
• Avoiding mixing chemicals

Any chemical exposure or spill must be reported immediately.

5. Reporting Incidents

All injuries, near-misses, hazards, or unsafe conditions must be reported to management immediately. Incident reports must be completed for any injury or safety-related event.
